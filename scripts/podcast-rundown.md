**SEGMENT 2 — Is the spend showing up in the earnings? (3:30–8:00)** ← expanded
- Size the bet: combined 2026 AI capex guidance is **~$725B** (Microsoft ~$190B + Alphabet ~$185B + Amazon ~$200B + Meta ~$130B) — up **~77% from ~$410B in 2025.** (Note: the "$452B" floating around is roughly the 2025 actual — use the ramp.)
- The one idea to teach viewers first: **capex is cash today, earnings tomorrow.** The cash leaves now, but the cost only hits the income statement slowly as *depreciation* over ~5–6 years. So look in 3 places, in order: revenue → margins → free cash flow.
- 🖥️ SCREEN-SHARE: scroll to the **"Is the AI capex showing up in earnings?"** deep-dive — walk the 4 charts:
  - **Revenue (yes, accelerating):** Azure +40%, Google Cloud +63%, AWS +28% (fastest in 15 quarters), Meta ads +33%, Nvidia Data Center +92%. Demand is real and capacity-constrained.
  - **Earnings/margins (holding, even rising):** operating income +~30% at all four; margins ~46% MSFT, ~41% Meta, 36% Alphabet, record 13% Amazon. Depreciation hasn't bitten yet.
  - **Free cash flow (the squeeze):** MSFT −22%, Alphabet $10.1B (from $19.0B), Amazon TTM −95%. This is where the bill shows up now — and what spooked the market last week.
  - **The other side:** the capex IS Nvidia's revenue — Data Center +92%, ~75% gross margin. Proof the dollars are real (and a bit circular).
- Land it: **yes, it's showing up — in revenue now and still in profit — but it's paid for in cash, and the real test (does the revenue cover the depreciation? i.e. ROIC) is ~12–18 months out.** Profits vs promises; unlike dot-com, these are real, profitable businesses — the debate is the timeline.
