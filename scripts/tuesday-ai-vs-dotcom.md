TOPIC: Everyone's calling AI "dot-com 2.0." The data says it's a different animal — with one real catch.

HOOK VARIANTS (pick one):

SELECTED HOOK: #2 — sets up the balanced structure and the payoff ("the 1 way") holds retention to the end.

POINT 1 — In 2000, the leaders had no earnings. Today they print money.
- Cisco — the Nvidia of 1999 — traded at a ~109x P/E on $18.9B in FULL-YEAR sales. Nvidia just did $81.6B in ONE quarter, up 85% year over year, at a ~47x P/E (company filings).
- Why it matters: a multiple can compress and the company survives. No earnings + no multiple = pets.com.
- Anchor: Nvidia's data-center segment alone ($75.2B/qtr) is 4x Cisco's entire best year.

POINT 2 — Dot-com ran on borrowed money. This one runs on cash flow.
- The telecom buildout was debt + vendor financing — Lucent and Nortel lent startups money to buy their own gear. When credit stopped, everything stopped.
- Today: Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta are funding ~$725B of 2026 capex mostly out of profits from ads, cloud, and e-commerce — businesses that make money whether AI pays off or not.
- Why it matters: cash-funded buildouts can slow down. Debt-funded buildouts blow up.

POINT 3 — The earnings actually showed up this time.
- March 2000: Nasdaq-100 at ~60x forward earnings, tech's share of market value way ahead of its share of profits.
- Now: the Mag-7 trade around ~38x forward — elevated, not 1999 — and tech generates roughly 20% of ALL S&P 500 profits. The weight is backed by earnings weight.
- Why it matters: the gap between price and profit is the bubble. That gap is much smaller today.

THE CATCH (the honest close — don't skip this):
- Capex is now eating ~94% of hyperscaler operating cash flow, vs ~40% in 2023 — and they've started issuing debt again (~$136B this year already, per BIS).
- The industry is spending roughly $8–10 for every $1 of current AI revenue. Circular deals (Nvidia funds OpenAI → OpenAI buys Nvidia chips) echo the vendor-financing playbook.
- Last week the BIS named an AI capex bust a top risk to the global financial system. "Different from 1999" ≠ "can't correct."

CLOSE + CTA
- Takeaway: this isn't dot-com 2.0 — the earnings are real. But watch the FUNDING, not the hype. The day this buildout runs on debt instead of cash flow is the day the comparison gets fair.
