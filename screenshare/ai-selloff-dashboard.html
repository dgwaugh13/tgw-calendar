Spending vs payoff — still the real story

Why the tape whipsaws both ways

The spend

~$725B of 2026 capex guidance across Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon and Meta (up from ~$410B in 2025) — data centers, chips, power.

The payoff question

Last week the market wanted proof; today it gave the benefit of the doubt. These firms are profitable — so sentiment, not solvency, drives the swings. The earnings deep-dive below shows where the proof actually is.

Segment 2 · Is the AI capex showing up in the earnings?

First, the one idea that explains everything: capex is cash today, earnings tomorrow

When these companies spend on data centers and chips, it does not hit the profit line all at once. The cash leaves immediately, but on the income statement the cost is spread out as depreciation over the useful life of the gear (~5–6 years). So if you want to know whether the AI bet is working, you have to look in three different places, and they tell the story in this order:

1 · Revenue — shows up first
If AI demand is real, you see it in cloud/AI sales right now. This is the leading signal.
2 · Operating margin — lags
Only a slice of the spend is expensed yet, so margins can still look great while depreciation ramps.
3 · Free cash flow — hit now
Cash out the door today. This is where a big capex bet shows its cost first and most clearly.
The short answer
In revenue?
Yes — accelerating
Every cloud/AI line re-accelerated last quarter. Demand is real and capacity-constrained.
In earnings/margins?
Holding — even rising
Operating income grew ~30% and margins held or expanded. Depreciation hasn't bitten yet.
In free cash flow?
Getting squeezed
FCF is down hard across the spenders — the cost is being paid in cash, now.
The real test (ROIC)?
~12–18 mo out
When today's builds finish and depreciate, does the AI revenue cover them? Too early to call.

Analyst bottom line for the episode

So — is the ~$725B showing up in earnings? Yes, where you'd expect it first. Revenue is accelerating (cloud +28% to +63%, Nvidia +92%), and profits are still growing ~30% with margins holding. The cost is real and it's hitting free cash flow right now, which is down sharply across the board. The one thing nobody can prove yet is the long-run return: when these data centers finish and the depreciation fully lands on the income statement, does the AI revenue cover it? That's the ~12–18-month question. Today's verdict: working on the top line, paid for in cash, with the real test still ahead.
